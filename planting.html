Why Planting Trees is Important

Planting trees is vital to maintaining the balance of the ecosystem. Trees provide oxygen, improve air quality, conserve water, and support wildlife. By planting more trees, you contribute to:

  • Combating climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.
  • Improving air quality and reducing pollution.
  • Creating habitats for wildlife.
  • Preventing soil erosion and preserving water resources.

Tree Planting Tips

Here are some tips for planting and caring for trees:

  • Choose native species to support the local ecosystem.
  • Plant trees in early spring or fall for the best results.
  • Water regularly, especially during the first year after planting.
  • Mulch around the base to retain moisture and prevent weeds.
  • Avoid planting too close to buildings or power lines.
